Our goals for 2030

We envision a future where coral reefs thrive, ecosystems flourish, and communities coexist sustainably with nature. Our commitment extends to the year 2030, where we have set ambitious goals to make a lasting impact on the health and preservation of coral reefs.

Coral restoration excellence

By 2030, establish Coral Restoration Excellence programs across key coral reef locations, aiming to rehabilitate and restore at least 50% of degraded coral ecosystems, fostering biodiversity and resilience.

Community empowerment

Launch the Community Empowerment Initiative to actively involve local communities in coral reef conservation. Through education and capacity-building programs, empower at least 20 coastal communities to become guardians of their marine environments.

Global advocacy campaign

Lead a Global Advocacy Campaign to raise awareness about the critical state of coral reefs and the interconnectedness of marine health with human well-being. Garner support for international policies that prioritize coral reef protection and sustainable ocean management.

Plastic-free oceans initiative

Launch the Plastic-Free Oceans Initiative to address the impact of marine plastic pollution on coral reefs. Implement programs to reduce plastic waste, conduct clean-up initiatives, and advocate for policies that minimize the use of single-use plastics, aiming for a 50% reduction in plastic pollution in coral reef areas.
